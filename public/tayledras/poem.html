Poem. . . set to flute music, along the line of common song of unrequited love;


What would I say if I had the words?
Why should I say anything at all?
If through not-action everything is done,
Through silence is all spoken?

What would I say if I had the words?
I dream in the wonders of music and verse
Yet daylight hours condemn me to prose
Is music only a waking dream?

What would I say if I had the words?
Would I tell you I loved you and find it true?
Does making it verbal make it real?
Should I say nothing to bind thee less?

What would I say if I had the words?
Would I tell of laughter and song?
Or would I spill woe all day long?
Perhaps silence is best. So now that the words are not allowed I find that I need them more than ever Life flows onward, me in the lee; And I, I say nothing. . . .
